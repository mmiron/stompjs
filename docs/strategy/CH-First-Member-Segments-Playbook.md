# First Member Segments Playbook (Ottawa Launch)

Date: March 3, 2026  
Purpose: Define best initial member types, onboarding approach, and segment tradeoffs

---

## 1) How to Pick First Segments

Use these selection criteria:
- High admin burden (invoicing, follow-ups, paperwork)
- Recurring service operations (repeatable workflow value)
- Clear legal/accounting/document needs
- Low customization required for v1 delivery
- Strong referral potential within local networks

Prioritize segments where Collective Hive can show measurable value in the first 30–60 days.

---

## 2) Recommended First Member Segments

## Segment A: General Construction / Renovation Contractors
### Why attractive
- Heavy invoicing and cashflow coordination needs
- Frequent document and compliance overhead
- Repeat vendor purchasing opportunities

### Service fit (v1)
- Invoice workflow and collections cadence support
- Bookkeeping readiness and document organization
- Contract/template intake routing

### Pros
- High pain = high perceived value
- Strong word-of-mouth in local trades
- Many repeatable workflows

### Cons
- Variable project cashflow can increase support complexity
- Busy owners may delay onboarding docs

### Onboarding tips
- 60-minute kickoff focused on current quote-to-cash process
- Import open invoices and aging first (quick win)
- Set weekly payment follow-up workflow in week 1

---

## Segment B: Roofing / Siding Specialists
### Why attractive
- Seasonal demand creates admin spikes
- Frequent estimates, job deposits, and invoice tracking
- High need for predictable records and partner-ready docs

### Service fit (v1)
- Estimate-to-invoice process consistency
- Document pack structure for accounting/legal requests
- Procurement support for materials/equipment quotes

### Pros
- Clear ROI from better invoice discipline
- Standardized service model easier to templatize

### Cons
- Seasonal volatility affects monthly predictability
- Peak-season support load can surge quickly

### Onboarding tips
- Build seasonal cashflow workflow at kickoff
- Preconfigure estimate/invoice templates by job type
- Implement “end-of-day job close” document checklist

---

## Segment C: Landscaping / Lawn Care
### Why attractive
- Recurring client billing cycles
- Route-based service businesses benefit from process standardization
- Good fit for basic web + booking + invoicing support

### Service fit (v1)
- Recurring invoicing cadence management
- Back-office task calendar and reminders
- Website/maintenance updates and service info hygiene

### Pros
- Predictable recurring workflow patterns
- Easier onboarding than project-based industries

### Cons
- Price-sensitive members may challenge plan fit
- Seasonal fluctuations in some service lines

### Onboarding tips
- Migrate recurring customer list and billing schedule first
- Set monthly reconciliation day and checklist
- Add weather/season exception process early

---

## Segment D: Snow Removal Services
### Why attractive
- High seasonal urgency and operational pressure
- Strong need for clean billing and documentation after events
- Potential procurement value (equipment/parts/vendor sourcing)

### Service fit (v1)
- Event-based billing workflow and rapid invoice turnaround
- Centralized service records for disputes and reconciliation
- Admin support during peak periods

### Pros
- Immediate pain relief in season
- Clear measurable outcomes (faster invoicing, fewer disputes)

### Cons
- Highly seasonal; off-season retention value must be maintained
- Support intensity can spike unpredictably

### Onboarding tips
- Pre-season onboarding (critical)
- Define storm-event documentation workflow upfront
- Prebuild invoice templates tied to service tiers/events

---

## Segment E: Small Trade Service Firms (Plumbing/Electrical/HVAC)
### Why attractive
- Constant service tickets and invoicing cycles
- Need legal/accounting support without full back-office staff

### Service fit (v1)
- Job-to-invoice admin flow
- Template/legal intake support
- Document readiness for accounting cycles

### Pros
- Year-round demand in many cases
- Strong repeatability in process design

### Cons
- Integration expectations may be higher
- Owners often overloaded and hard to schedule

### Onboarding tips
- Start with one service lane/process instead of full migration
- Prioritize aging receivables and quote close rate workflow

---

## 3) Suggested Segment Priority (Launch)

Recommended order for Ottawa pilot:
1. Landscaping / recurring local service firms
2. General contractors / renovation operators
3. Roofing / siding specialists
4. Snow removal (pre-season cohort strategy)

Reason: Start with operationally repeatable workflows, then add higher-volatility segments once support/SLA systems are stable.

---

## 4) Segment-Specific Onboarding Framework

For each segment, run the same 4-stage onboarding model:

### Stage 1: Baseline (Days 1–3)
- Current tools and workflow map
- Open invoices and cashflow snapshot
- Required documents and partner-routing setup

### Stage 2: Quick Wins (Days 4–10)
- Fix invoice cadence
- Implement reminders/follow-up rhythm
- Set recurring admin checklists

### Stage 3: Partner Integration (Days 11–20)
- Accounting/legal intake standardization
- SLA routing and escalation setup
- Compliance boundary confirmation

### Stage 4: Value Proof (Days 21–30)
- Report time saved and process stability gains
- Document cost savings opportunities
- Set next-30-day optimization plan

## 6) Recommended Launch Strategy by Segment

- Start with 1–2 segments only (avoid over-broad launch)
- Build one repeatable onboarding template per segment
- Track segment-level metrics separately:
  - onboarding duration
  - first 30-day outcome score
  - churn at 90 days
  - support intensity per member

Scale only segments with strong retention + margin + manageable support load.

---

## 7) Practical Suggestions (Actionable)

1. Run a 10-member pilot split across two segments max.
2. Build “segment playbooks” before adding the third segment.
3. Publish one case study per segment before scaling spend.
4. Use pre-season campaigns for seasonal segments (roofing/snow).
5. Avoid adding complex new services until segment workflows are stable.

---

## 8) Decision Prompt

Choose your first two target segments now and lock:
- Segment-specific onboarding checklist
- Segment-specific pricing guardrails
- Segment-specific success KPIs

This gives immediate focus and prevents scope dilution in the launch window.
