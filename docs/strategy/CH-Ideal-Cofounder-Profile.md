# Ideal Cofounder Profile (Collective Hive)

Date: March 3, 2026  
Purpose: Define the best-fit partner profile for Collective Hive’s next stage

## 1) Why This Role Exists

Collective Hive’s model is operationally complex (multi-service orchestration, partner governance, SLA discipline, and AI-enabled workflows). The ideal cofounder complements founder strengths by owning execution rigor and scalable delivery.

## 2) Recommended Role Type (Primary)

### Option A (Most Needed in this model): COO / Head of Operations
Owns:
- Onboarding and service delivery systems
- SOP quality and SLA management
- Partner operations governance (accounting/legal lanes)
- Monthly operating cadence and KPI integrity

### Option B (If ops is already covered): Product/Automation Cofounder
Owns:
- Workflow automation roadmap
- Internal ops platform/product layer
- Data pipeline for KPI and lender reporting
- AI-human handoff quality controls

## 3) Must-Have Capabilities

- Proven execution in service operations or scaled SMB ops
- Ability to build repeatable processes from ambiguity
- Strong operational numeracy (KPIs, margins, churn, queue metrics)
- Comfort running with lean resources
- High ownership and follow-through under uncertainty
- Excellent communication with both clients and partners

## 4) Nice-to-Have Capabilities

- Experience with partner-led regulated services (legal/accounting/insurance)
- Familiarity with workflow automation tooling (Make/n8n/Zapier style)
- Exposure to subscription businesses and unit economics
- Basic data modeling/reporting literacy

## 5) Values and Working Style Fit

Non-negotiables:
- Integrity and trustworthiness
- Low-ego, high-accountability behavior
- Bias toward action with measurable outcomes
- Respect for compliance boundaries and risk controls
- Long-term mindset (not quick-flip mentality)

## 6) Time and Commitment Expectations

Minimum expected commitment:
- Full-time or near-full-time equivalent effort
- Clear 12-month commitment horizon
- Weekly operating cadence and accountability reviews

## 7) Equity and Structure Principles (Discussion-Level)

- Equity tied to role criticality + real contribution + commitment duration
- Standard vesting expected (4 years with 1-year cliff)
- Milestone accountability should be explicit in founder plan
- Avoid fixed entitlement without performance and time commitment

## 8) Candidate Screening Questions

1. Tell me about a system you built that scaled without linear headcount growth.
2. How do you handle underperforming partners without breaking delivery?
3. Which operating metrics do you monitor weekly and why?
4. Describe a time you had to enforce process discipline under pressure.
5. How do you balance speed with compliance and quality?
6. What would your first 90 days at Collective Hive look like?

## 9) 30-Day Trial Collaboration Plan (Before Final Equity Terms)

Week 1:
- Co-design onboarding flow and operating cadence

Week 2:
- Build/upgrade one critical SOP lane and escalation rules

Week 3:
- Run a simulated or real partner-performance review process

Week 4:
- Present 90-day execution plan with KPI impact assumptions

Decision checkpoint:
- Continue / revise role / no-go

## 10) Red Flags (Do Not Ignore)

- Wants title/equity before contribution clarity
- Avoids measurable commitments
- Dismisses compliance and process controls
- Lacks stamina for operational detail
- Misaligned time horizon or risk appetite

## 11) Final Selection Checklist

- [ ] Complementary skill set to founder
- [ ] Values and decision-style alignment
- [ ] Demonstrated execution capability
- [ ] Willingness for vesting and accountability
- [ ] Strong 90-day plan quality
- [ ] Positive reference and trust validation
