## 1. Vision and Core Concept

Create a membership-based business platform that gives small businesses and solo operators access to services usually available to larger corporations.

**Core promise:**
- Remove business housekeeping burden
- Improve affordability of professional services
- Increase purchasing power through collective demand

**Positioning line:**
You run your business. We run the back office.

---

## 2. Problem Being Solved

Small and early-stage businesses are often constrained by:
- Admin overload (billing, invoicing, records, compliance prep)
- Expensive one-off legal/accounting access
- Fragmented vendors and tools
- Weak buying power compared with larger organizations

Desired outcome:
- One membership, one support desk, coordinated operations, measurable time and cost savings.

## 5. Membership Model and Terms

### Annual vs monthly decision
- Recommended: offer both monthly and annual plans.
- Annual is positioned as better value (discount + onboarding credits/bonuses).
- Monthly remains available to reduce signup friction.

### Why not force annual-only at launch
- New brand trust is still forming.
- Forced lock-in may reduce conversion.

### Suggested terms approach
- Monthly: higher rate, flexible cancel terms.
- Annual: lower effective rate, better included value.

## 7. Lean Launch Strategy

### Lean principle
Launch as a coordination + membership platform first, not a full in-house specialist operation.

### Lean launch service set (first 6–12 months)
- Invoicing/bookkeeping coordination
- Legal template + partner consult access
- Managed hosting/design support

### Team lean objective
- Keep core staff low
- Use licensed external partners
- Use AI + automation to reduce manual work

## 8. AI-First Operating Model

### AI role
AI acts as a copilot layer across repetitive operations; humans own approvals and regulated decisions.

### Immediate AI use cases
- Lead qualification and follow-up drafting
- Onboarding intake and missing-doc detection
- Helpdesk triage and response drafting
- Bookkeeping categorization suggestions and anomaly flags
- Legal intake structuring and clause comparison prep
- Procurement option comparison and savings estimate drafts

### Human-required zones
- Legal advice
- Accounting sign-off
- Insurance recommendations
- Final high-risk decisions

### Guardrails required
- Human-in-loop approval paths
- Audit logging
- Data retention and access controls
- Clear client-facing disclosures on AI-assisted workflows

## 10. People Plan and Org Scaling

### Initial lean staffing
- Founder/GM
- Ops/customer success generalist
- Technical automation builder (full-time or fractional)
- Fractional legal/compliance and finance advisors

### Member milestone staffing map
- **50 members:** ~2–3 core + partners
- **250 members:** ~5–7 core + expanded partner bench
- **1,000 members:** ~10–16 core + functional ops/support roles
- **5,000 members:** ~30–60 core if automation is strong

### Design principle
Headcount should not scale linearly with members; standardized workflows and partner leverage are required.

## 13. Funds Flow, Umbrella Model, and Regulatory Guardrails

### Initial member-funds concept discussed
The idea of centralizing all billing/invoicing/receipts and issuing “dividends” for members was explored.

### Key caution
This can trigger significant regulatory, tax, fiduciary, and compliance complexity in Canada, especially around:
- Client money handling
- Commingling risks
- Securities/co-op implications
- Employment/contractor classification

### Safer v1 operating architecture
- Members keep their own legal entities and bank accounts.
- Platform centralizes workflows and data orchestration.
- Funds move via licensed payment rails.
- Value return through fee rebates/credits/discounts instead of dividend-like structures early.

### Umbrella corporation model discussion
Possible in principle, but substantially higher legal/operational burden and not recommended as first operating model.

### Targets discussed

- CAC payback < 4 months (goal)

## B) Corporate member count vs yearly profit (illustrative)

- 250 members: near break-even / small loss in conservative modeling
