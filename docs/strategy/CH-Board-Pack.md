# Collective Hive Board Pack (Draft)

**Date:** March 3, 2026  
**Company:** Collective Hive  
**Stage:** Early validation / pre-scale  
**Region:** Ottawa, Ontario (initial)

---

## 1) Executive Summary

Collective Hive is building a membership-based operating platform for small businesses and solo operators. The business combines AI-assisted workflow execution, standardized operating procedures, and partner-delivered professional services to remove operational burden and improve cost efficiency.

**Core proposition:**
- One membership
- One support desk
- One coordinated back-office operating layer

**Near-term focus:**
- Validate repeatable onboarding and retention in Ottawa
- Prove unit economics under controlled scope
- Build lender/investor-ready operating discipline

---

## 2) Problem and Opportunity

### Problem
Small businesses experience:
- Admin overload
- Fragmented providers and systems
- Expensive ad hoc legal/accounting access
- Poor purchasing leverage

### Opportunity
A unified membership model can create measurable value by:
- Reducing owner time spent on admin
- Improving service consistency
- Lowering operating costs through standardization and partner leverage

---

## 3) Product and Service Scope (Current)

### Included in current operating scope
- Invoicing and bookkeeping coordination
- Legal template access + discounted partner consult routing
- Managed web/hosting operational support
- Procurement support and member savings workflows

### Explicitly out of current scope
- Becoming an insurance carrier
- High-risk pooled client funds models

### Delivery model
- AI handles repetitive workflow tasks
- Humans own approvals and regulated decisions
- Licensed partners deliver regulated services

---

## 4) Go-To-Market Plan

### Initial market
- Ottawa solo operators and small companies (1–25 employees)

### Channel strategy
- Founder-led direct sales
- Partner referrals (accounting/legal ecosystem)
- Verticalized outreach by niche
- Referral loop with member incentives

### First milestones
- 10–20 paying members in first launch window
- 50+ members with stable onboarding quality and SLA performance
- Measurable case studies demonstrating hours and cost savings

---

## 5) Operating System and KPIs

### KPI groups
- Growth: active members, net adds, churn
- Unit economics: ARPM, gross margin, CAC, CAC payback
- Service quality: response times, resolution times, SLA breaches
- Cash health: ending cash, runway, net monthly cash change

### Governance cadence
- Weekly service review
- Monthly operating review (MOR)
- Quarterly strategy recalibration

---

## 6) 24-Month Financial Scenario Summary

### Low Case (stress)
- Slower growth, lower ARPM, weaker retention
- Negative cash trajectory by month 24 unless corrective action/capital

### Base Case (planning default)
- Controlled growth and improving margins
- Near EBITDA break-even by end of Year 2
- Positive cash retained in current assumptions

### High Case (upside)
- Faster growth + stronger retention + improved margins
- Strong Year 2 EBITDA and cash rebuild

### Board view
Base case should remain primary planning baseline, with low/high used for risk and capacity planning.

---

## 7) Risk and Control Summary

### Top strategic risks
1. Churn and value mismatch
2. Partner SLA inconsistency
3. CAC efficiency deterioration
4. Regulatory boundary errors in regulated workflows
5. Cash runway compression during scaling

### Control response
- Strict service boundary policy
- Partner scorecards and redundancy
- KPI-triggered hiring and spend controls
- Monthly covenant-style risk thresholds

---

## 8) Team and Hiring Philosophy

### Lean-by-design model
- Keep core team lean
- Scale via automation and partner capacity
- Add internal roles only at objective trigger thresholds

### Hiring triggers already defined
- Member growth thresholds
- Ticket load thresholds
- Exception and compliance load thresholds
